SCIENCE PLATFORM
A Synthesis of Nobel Ideas

qFoldIT is a platform built on discoveries awarded the Nobel Prize, continuing the legacy of FoldIT — turning complex science into engaging gameplay accessible to everyone.

qFoldIT combines quantum algorithms, AI, and crowdsourcing in a gaming environment to redefine molecular design and climate challenges. Following the legacy of FoldIT and Nobel laureates, we transform cutting-edge science into an interactive adventure with high-performance molecular visualization.

From Complex Games to Living Room Fun

qFoldIT reimagines the gaming paradigm: it steps away from solitary, hyper‑complex simulators and returns to the warmth of shared experiences. Like classic board games that once gathered families around a table, qFoldIT turns molecular science into a collaborative adventure — parents and children solving protein folds, exploring Arctic climate scenarios, or designing new molecules side by side. It is not merely a game; it is a new genre of science entertainment designed for the living room, where discovery becomes a family ritual.

Physics 2024: AI as a Tool for Discovery

Awarded to John Hopfield and Geoffrey Hinton for foundational AI discoveries. qFoldIT uses Hopfield networks (associative memory) and deep learning for adaptive AI assistants, procedural generation of Arctic landscapes, and molecular structure prediction.

Chemistry 2024: Computational Protein Design

David Baker (de novo protein design) and Demis Hassabis / John Jumper (AlphaFold) opened the era of computational biology. qFoldIT advances both: gamified molecular design and quantum‑accelerated peptide folding, exploring conformational space at a new level.

2007 Nobel Peace Prize: Climate & Sustainability

The IPCC and Al Gore highlighted the link between climate and global security. qFoldIT is the digital twin of the Arctic station “Snezhinka” (AHEAD project, Arctic Council). Players immerse in climate challenges, design carbon‑free technologies, and discover enzymes for bioremediation.

Industry 4.0 & Synthetic Biology

The Fourth Industrial Revolution (term coined at Hannover Fair 2011) is based on the convergence of digital, physical, and biological worlds. qFoldIT embodies its core principle: digital modelling of biological systems (DNA → computer → “living factory”). This enables drugs tailored to genetic diversity and DNA‑based data storage.

qFoldIT follows the FoldIT tradition: a community of player‑researchers solves real scientific problems, while quantum algorithms and AI push the boundaries of what is possible.
